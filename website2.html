High-Speed Railway October 1, 1964, Japan Tokaido Shinkansen officially opened to traffic. Operating speeds of up to 210 km/h. So the world's first real sense of high-speed railway was invented by Japan. This marks the arrival of a new era of high-speed railways in the world. As a pioneer of high-speed railways in the world, Japan has accumulated rich experience for other countries that have not yet developed or are developing high-speed railways, including China, and has transferred some technologies to these countries for a fee to help them develop high-speed railway systems faster and more conveniently. In March 2006, the CRH2A train, which had been converted from the E2 series 1000 x, was shipped from the port of Kobe to Qingdao, China, and part of the technology was transferred to China, thus opening the high-speed rail era in China. scan to pay The pay-per-code model is based on the concept of mobile payments, and the first payments made by mobile devices took place in Finland in 1997. Sharing a bike As early as 1965, the Dutch city of Amsterdam proposed the "White Project", according to which the government purchased 50 bicycles and painted them with white paint as marks to be scattered around the city for people to use. This was the world's first unmanned bike-sharing system, so shared bikes were invented in the Netherlands. In 2007, France also had free cycling, and only later did China become popular and develop innovative models, and promote overseas. Online shopping Online shopping belongs to the category of e-commerce. In 1979, an Englishman named Michael Aldrich invented the concept of online shopping. Using a technology known as Videotex, Aldrich connected regular television sets to computers at local retailers via telephone lines. By the 1990s, e-commerce became popular worldwide after Amazon and eBay launched their websites in 1995.

Two-dimensional Barcode Payment

Two-dimensional code payment is a new generation of wireless payment scheme based on the account system. Under the payment scheme, merchants can compile transaction information such as account numbers and commodity prices into a two-dimensional code, and print it on various newspapers, magazines, advertisements, books and other carriers to publish. The user can scan the QR code through the mobile phone client or the merchant can scan the user's payment code with the electronic payment tool to realize the payment settlement with the merchant's account. Finally, merchants can carry out commodity distribution and complete the transaction according to the user's receipt and contact information in the payment transaction information. At the same time, because many two-dimensional code scanning tools do not have the ability to identify and intercept malicious websites, Tencent mobile phone butler data shows that this gives mobile phone viruses a great space to spread, for online malicious websites, payment environment scanning and detection to avoid two-dimensional code scanning channel exposure. On August 3, 2016, the Payment and Clearing Association issued the Barcode Payment Business Specification (draft for public comments) to payment institutions, which clearly pointed out the safety standards that payment institutions should follow when carrying out barcode business. This is the first official recognition of QR code payments since the central bank halted the practice in 2014.
