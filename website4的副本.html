Different kinds of music around the world

1.Kpop

The literal translation of KPOP is Korean pop music, which is a commercial music genre that integrates European and American pop music elements with Korean traditional folk music. Its development direction has been closely following the European and American pop music, but because of the localization change speed later than the European and American pop music for a period of time; But this trend is now waning and more companies are taking pre-emptive measures (such as directly inviting European and American producers to orchestrate or sample less popular music). Only by exceeding popularity can we make popularity, and making popularity is higher than imitating popularity. This is the high generalization of the development of KPOP.

2.Pop

Pop Music, translated from English Popular Music. According to the superficial understanding of Chinese words, the so-called popular music refers to those instrumental music and songs with short structure, popular content, lively form and sincere emotion, which are loved by the broad masses, widely sung or appreciated, popular and even passed down to later generations. These music and songs are rooted in the rich soil of public life. Therefore, it is also known as "popular music".

3.Jazz

During the 19th century, music was an important way for black plantation slaves in the southern United States to express their lives and feelings. Since the late 19th century, jazz has been based on traditional British and American music, mixed with blues, ragtime and other genres, and is a hybrid product. Black music in the Americas retains a lot of African characteristics, rhythmic characteristics, and retains the characteristics of collective improvisation. This tradition was combined with the music -- mostly vocal -- of the new place, and the result was not just a new sound but an entirely new form of musical

4.Classical

Classical music is a broad term, the broad Western classical music refers to those from the Western Middle Ages, and in the context of the European mainstream culture, or refers to the roots of Western traditional liturgical music and secular music, covering the entire period around the 9th century AD. It is different from popular music and folk music mainly because of its complex and diverse creative techniques and the heavy connotations it can carry. Geographically, the music was composed mainly in Europe and the Americas, as opposed to non-Western music. In addition, Western classical music is mainly recorded and transmitted by sheet music, which is different from the oral mode of most folk music.
